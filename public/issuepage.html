HR 45 - Blair Holt's Firearm Licensing and Record of Sale Act of 2009

Summary

Introduced in January 6, 2009, by Representative Bobby Rush (D) Illinois, Blair Holt's Firearm Licensing and Record of Sale Act of 2009, known as HR 45, amends the Brady Handgun Violence Prevention Act. The Act would establish a nationwide system for prohibiting unlicensed gun-ownership. If approved, the law would require gun owners to apply for five-year licenses to own firearms, and would give the U.S. Attorney General broad authority over the program. The Act, which directs the Attorney General to establish and maintain a federal record of sale system, prescribes firearms transfer reporting and record keeping requirements.

Status of legislation

The legislation was referred to House Subcommittee on Crime, Terrorism, and Homeland Security, on February 2, 2009.
